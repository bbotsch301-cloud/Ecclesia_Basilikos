Tax Documentation Checklist

Annual Record-Keeping Requirements for Lawful Money Redeemers
12 U.S.C. § 411 • 26 U.S.C. § 61 • FRE 803(6)

Section 1: Core Documentation

  • Lawful Money Redemption Ledger — Complete for the full tax year, with running totals, all entries dated and sourced Supports: FRE 803(6) contemporaneous business record
  • Copies of All Endorsed Checks — Front and back, showing the restrictive endorsement language Authority: UCC §§ 3-206, 4-205
  • Bank Statements (all 12 months) — For every account receiving lawful money deposits Corroborates ledger entries
  • Annual Affidavit of Lawful Money Demand — Signed and dated (notarized if possible), covering the full year Authority: 28 U.S.C. § 1746
  • Standing Demand Letter to Bank — Copy of letter sent to bank (certified mail), requesting all deposits be treated as lawful money Authority: 12 U.S.C. § 411
  • Certified Mail Receipts — Green cards (return receipts) for all correspondence sent to bank Proves delivery and date of notice

Section 2: Bank Correspondence

  • Bank Response Letters — Any written responses from your bank regarding your lawful money demand
  • Account Opening Documents — If you opened a new account specifically for lawful money deposits
  • Electronic Payment Processor Records — Screenshots of address line 2 or notes field showing lawful money language
  • Direct Deposit Authorization Forms — Copies with lawful money stipulation noted

Section 3: Tax Filing Documentation

  • W-2 Forms — All W-2s received from employers for the tax year Shows income reported and tax withheld
  • 1099 Forms — All 1099s (1099-MISC, 1099-NEC, 1099-INT, etc.) for the tax year
  • Copy of Filed 1040 — Your completed tax return for the year
  • Summary Calculation — Showing total lawful money redeemed vs. total income reported, with reconciliation
  • Professional Consultation Records — Notes or correspondence with any tax professional consulted

Section 4: Year-End Summary

  • Annual Summary Statement — Total deposits redeemed, total by category (payroll, business income, other), running total from prior years
  • Ledger Reconciled to Bank Statements — Verify ledger totals match actual bank statement deposits
  • All Records Organized and Filed — Physical binder or digital folder, clearly labeled by year
Important: This checklist does not constitute legal or tax advice. Consult a qualified tax professional before filing. The lawful money redemption strategy does not address Social Security or Medicare (FICA) taxes. Be aware of the 26 U.S.C. § 6702 frivolous return penalty ($5,000). Ensure all filings are made in good faith with proper documentation.

Annual Timeline

1
January — Begin new year's ledger. Confirm standing demand letter is current with bank. Order new stamp ink if needed.
2
Monthly — Reconcile ledger to bank statements. File endorsed check copies. Note any electronic deposits with documentation.
3
December — Prepare year-end summary. Calculate total redeemed. Sign and notarize annual affidavit.
4
January-April (following year) — File tax return with professional guidance. Retain all records indefinitely.
Retention Period: Retain all lawful money records indefinitely. The IRS can audit up to 3 years (6 years if substantial understatement), but maintaining a complete historical record strengthens your position. Store originals in a secure, climate-controlled location. Keep digital backups (scanned copies) in a separate location.
