Endorsement Template & Guide

Lawful Money Redemption Under 12 U.S.C. § 411

The Endorsement Language

When depositing any check, paycheck, or negotiable instrument, write the following endorsement on the back of the check before deposit. No special tools are required — simply handwrite it in the endorsement area. For convenience, you may optionally order a rubber stamp, but a stamp is not necessary.

Deposited For Credit On Account.
Redeemed In Lawful Money
Per 12 U.S.C. § 411
by: _____________________________, grantor,
all rights reserved
Exact Endorsement Language
Red Ink Recommended: It is best (but not mandatory) for the endorsement to be in red ink. Red ink visually distinguishes your lawful money demand from a standard signature endorsement and creates a clear record that your intent was stated at the time of deposit.

Where to Place the Endorsement

Back of Check — Endorsement Area
Deposited For Credit On Account.
Redeemed In Lawful Money
Per 12 U.S.C. § 411
by: John Henry Doe, grantor,
all rights reserved

Place the endorsement in the standard endorsement area on the back of the check (the top 1.5 inches). Your signature may appear above, below, or integrated with the stamp language.

Optional: Ordering a Custom Stamp

If you prefer the convenience of a stamp (not required), order one from any office supply or online stamp vendor. Specifications:

  1. Size: Approximately 1.5" x 2.5" (fits standard check endorsement area)
  2. Ink Color: Red (order a red ink pad separately if the stamp is self-inking)
  3. Font: Clear, legible font (Arial, Times New Roman, or similar) at 8-10pt
  4. Text: Use the exact language shown above, with your full name in the "by:" line
  5. Cost: Typically $15-30 for a self-inking stamp

For Electronic / Direct Deposits

If you receive funds electronically (direct deposit, ACH, wire transfer), you cannot physically endorse a check. Instead:

  1. Open a dedicated bank account and stipulate in writing that all incoming funds are received as lawful money per 12 U.S.C. § 411
  2. Payment processors: Go into the backend where your address is listed and type into the second address line: "All funds received as lawful money per 12 U.S.C. 411"
  3. Send a Standing Demand Letter to your bank (certified mail, return receipt) stating your ongoing demand for lawful money on all deposits
  4. The point: Have it ON RECORD that you are redeeming lawful money, so if audited, you can clearly demonstrate your position

Handling Bank Teller Objections

Objection Your Response
"We can't accept this endorsement" "Are you dishonoring the check? You are required to honor the endorsement under the UCC and 12 U.S.C. § 411. Please process it or get a manager."
"What does this mean?" "It's a lawful money demand under federal statute. It simply means I want my deposit credited as lawful money rather than private credit. It doesn't change anything on your end."
"I need to check with my manager" "That's fine. The statute is 12 U.S.C. § 411. Your compliance department will be familiar with it."
"We've never seen this before" "It's a federal statute that's been in effect since 1913. I'm exercising my right under it. Please process the deposit."
Key Principle: Under Parsons Laws of Business, p. 172: "Bank notes are a good tender unless objected to at the time because not money." Anything is acceptable as tender unless objected to. By endorsing your checks, you are making your objection known on the record. Silence is acquiescence.
