Key Definitions

  • Lawful MoneyCurrency authorized by law a creditor must accept (Black's 11th)
  • Legal TenderMoney legally valid for payment of debts (31 USC § 5103)
  • FRN (Obligation)An obligation of the U.S. issued through the Fed (12 USC § 411)
  • RedemptionDemanding lawful money in exchange for FRNs (12 USC § 411)
  • Private CreditCredit system of the Federal Reserve (Federal Reserve Act § 16)

Supreme Court Cases

  • Bronson v. Rodes (1868)Coin and notes are not equivalents
  • Hepburn v. Griswold (1870)Legal Tender Act unconstitutional (later partially reversed)
  • Knox v. Lee (1871)Legal tender power upheld for wartime
  • Juilliard v. Greenman (1884)Congress has power to issue legal tender
  • Perry v. U.S. (1935)No authority to alter promise of payment
  • Eisner v. Macomber (1920)Income = gain from capital or labor
  • Glenshaw Glass (1955)Income = accession to wealth, clearly realized
  • Veazie Bank v. Fenno (1869)Congress power to provide national currency

Circuit / District Court Cases

  • Milam v. U.S. (9th Cir. 1974)FRN is an obligation, not a dollar
  • Capitol Grain v. Fed Reserve (1925)Non gold/silver payment unconstitutional
  • The Edith (1875)Court decrees must be in lawful money
  • Forbes v. Murray (1869)Recovery must be in gold/silver coins

  • Lane v. Railey; U.S. v. WellsMoney does not embrace notes

  • U.S. v. GellmanMoney is a commodity with intrinsic value
